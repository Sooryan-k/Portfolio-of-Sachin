Sachin Ramesh Tendulkar







Here is a time line of Sachin's life:



  • 1973 - Born in Mumbai, India
  • 1987 - The 14-year-old Tendulkar was selected to represent Bombay in the Ranji Trophy
  • 1989 - Tendulkar made his Test debut against Pakistan in Karachi
  • 1990 - He became the second youngest cricketer to score a Test century
  • 1992 - At the age of 19, Tendulkar became the first overseas-born player to represent Yorkshire
  • 1994 - He scored his first ODI century against Australia in Sri Lanka at Colombo
  • 1996 - Tendulkar took over as captain
  • 1996 - Tendulkar's rise continued when he was the leading run scorer at the 1996 World Cup
  • 1998 - Tendulkar scored three consecutive centuries in Australian tour of India
  • 2003 - Cricket World Cup, RSA: Sachin Tendulkar, Player of Series
  • 2008 - Sachin Tendulkar becomes the highest run-scorer ever in test cricket beating Brian Lara's record and passing 12,000 runs against Australia in Mohali
  • 2010 - Sachin Tendulkar scores the first double century in One Day International cricket
  • 2010 - IPL Cricket Final, DY Patil Stadium, Mumbai: Chennai Super Kings beat Mumbai Indians by 22 runs; Suresh Raina 57 no (35)
  • 2011 - India won ICC Cricket World Cup 2011
  • 2012 - Indian batsman Sachin Tendulkar becomes first cricketer to score 100 international centuries
  • 2013 - Sachin Tendulkar plays his very last cricket match before retiring in Mumbai's Wankhede Stadium against the West Indies
